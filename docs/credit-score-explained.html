The Building Blocks of Your Score

Your score is calculated from several key pieces of your financial history. Paying bills on time is by far the most important factor.

Understanding this breakdown helps you focus your efforts on what matters most for building strong credit.

Your Action Plan for a Better Score

1. Pay Bills On Time, Every Time

This is the single most important factor. Set up auto-pay to never miss a due date.

2. Keep Credit Card Balances Low

Aim to use less than 30% of your available credit limit. Lower is always better.

3. Build a Long Credit History

Avoid closing old credit cards, as a longer history shows stability and can boost your score.

4. Limit New Credit Applications

Applying for too much credit in a short time can be a red flag. Only apply when you need it.

5. Check Your Credit Report

Request free reports annually to check for errors that could be hurting your score and dispute them.
